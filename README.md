## 📌 Software Overview
Android Data Recovery Pro allows users to restore deleted files from Android mobile devices. This software works on Windows 10 and Windows 11 computers. It finds lost photos, messages, contacts, and documents. The tool connects to your device via USB to scan for data that you accidentally deleted. 

## ⚙️ System Requirements
Before you start, ensure your computer meets these requirements:
*   A computer running Windows 10 or Windows 11.
*   At least 2GB of available hard drive space.
*   An active USB port to connect your phone.
*   A USB data cable for your specific Android model.
*   Latest version of Java installed on your system.

## 📲 Setting Up Your Phone
The software communicates with your phone through a specific mode called USB Debugging. You must enable this for the software to see your files.
1. Open "Settings" on your Android device.
2. Navigate to "About Phone" and tap the "Build Number" seven times until you see a message saying you are a developer.
3. Return to the main "Settings" menu and find "Developer Options."
4. Ensure the toggle for "USB Debugging" is set to the ON position.
5. Connect your device to the computer using a high-quality USB cable.

## 🚀 Running the Recovery Process
Once the software is installed and your phone is connected, you can begin the recovery process.
1. Launch the program from the icon on your desktop.
2. Wait for the software to recognize your connected device. You might see a prompt on your phone screen asking to allow computer access. Tap "Allow."
3. Select the types of files you wish to recover from the provided list. These options include photos, videos, call logs, and text messages.
4. Click the "Scan" button to let the application search your device storage.
5. Review the list of found items once the scan finishes.
6. Check the box next to the files you want to keep.
7. Click the "Recover" button to save these items to your computer.

## 📁 Managing Recovered Data
The software saves files into a folder named "RecoveredData" under your Documents directory by default. You can change this path in the settings menu before you start the scan. Always check your files after recovery to ensure they opened correctly. If a file does not open, try running a deep scan instead of a quick scan.

## 💡 Troubleshooting Tips
If you experience issues, try these steps:
*   Use a different USB cable if the computer does not detect your phone.
*   Restart both your computer and your phone.
*   Close other programs that might try to access the phone at the same time.
*   Ensure that you are using the latest version of the software.
*   Check that your antivirus program does not block the software performance.
*   Verify that your Android phone has enough battery life to complete the scan.

## 🛡️ Privacy and Safety
This software runs locally on your PC. It does not upload your personal data to remote servers. All recovery operations stay within the safety of your computer's hard drive. Keep the software updated to maintain compatibility with new Android security patches and software releases.
